# OctoAcme Project Management Knowledge Base

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management Documentation! This hub centralizes our project management knowledge, standards, and best practices so that every team member—new or experienced—has equal access to the workflows, templates, and rationale that guide how we deliver. By bringing these docs together in one place (and into this Copilot Space), we transform institutional knowledge into searchable, versioned guidance that accelerates onboarding, reduces reliance on tribal knowledge, and ensures consistent, repeatable execution across all squads.

## Project Management Processes Summary

OctoAcme follows a structured, lifecycle-based approach to project management that emphasizes customer value, iterative delivery, and clear ownership. Our methodology is grounded in five foundational principles: **customer-first** prioritization, **iterative** delivery of small testable increments, **clear ownership** with a named Project Manager and Product Lead on every project, **data-informed** decision-making, and a culture of **psychological safety** that encourages continuous feedback and learning. These principles guide every phase of the project lifecycle, from initial problem framing through post-release retrospective.

The lifecycle spans five core phases. **Initiation** validates business need, aligns stakeholders, and captures success criteria in a project one-pager. **Planning** decomposes work into shippable increments with a Definition of Done, maps dependencies and risks, and builds an actionable roadmap. **Execution** drives iterative delivery through daily standups, PR reviews with quality gates, automated CI/CD, and a three-level escalation ladder to resolve blockers quickly. **Release** enforces pre-deployment verification—acceptance criteria completion, passing CI and security scans, release notes, and smoke test preparation—before any launch. Finally, **Close & Retrospective** runs structured 45–75 minute sessions to capture "what went well / what to improve," converting learnings into tracked action items that close the loop on continuous improvement.

Responsibilities are distributed across four primary personas. **Project Managers** coordinate schedules, manage risks and dependencies, and maintain transparency through status reporting and escalation protocols. **Product Managers** define what should be built, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes through success metrics. **Developers** implement features while maintaining code quality, writing tests, and participating in design reviews. **QA/Testing** validates quality against acceptance criteria. Cross-functional alignment is maintained through weekly PM-to-PdM syncs, twice-weekly team standups, monthly stakeholder updates, and ad-hoc escalations. Risk is managed via a Risk Register (ID, Description, Impact, Likelihood, Owner, Mitigation) reviewed weekly, and teams use GitHub Projects with standardized columns (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done) and velocity/burndown metrics to keep delivery on track.
